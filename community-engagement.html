What is a Mosquito?

Introduction

Mosquitoes are small, flying insects belonging to the family Culicidae. They are known for their slender bodies, long legs, and distinctive proboscis used for feeding.

Biology & Lifecycle

Mosquitoes undergo complete metamorphosis: egg, larva, pupa, and adult. Most species lay eggs in stagnant water, where the larvae and pupae develop before emerging as adults.

Role in Ecosystem

Mosquitoes serve as food for many creatures such as birds, bats, and fish. However, they are most notable for their role as vectors of diseases such as malaria, dengue, and Zika.

Why Study Mosquitoes?

Understanding mosquitoes helps us develop better strategies for controlling diseases and protecting public health. Research covers biology, behavior, and innovative control methods.
